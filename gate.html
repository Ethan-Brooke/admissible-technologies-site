Winding-class holonomic gates

Every quantum gate is an instruction the control electronics deliver imperfectly — and in a normal gate, an imperfect pulse is a coherent mistake the qubit faithfully executes. The winding-class gate breaks that link: the operation is fixed by an integer, so amplitude error largely cancels by construction.

The problem: gates inherit every control error.

In a conventional gate, the rotation angle is proportional to the drive amplitude and duration. Get the pulse 1% wrong and the gate is 1% wrong — a coherent error that accumulates across a circuit faster than decoherence does. You can calibrate it away, but only momentarily, and only per device.

How it works: a loop in a dark subspace.

The logical qubit lives in a two-state dark subspace — a pair of zero-energy states decoupled from the lossy excited level. Sweeping flux through the circuit's loops steers that subspace around a closed path, and the resulting non-Abelian (Wilczek–Zee) holonomy depends only on the integer winding number of the path. The gate angle is geometry, not amplitude — and the connection that generates it is constant by gauge invariance, which is why a single Josephson junction per loop is load-bearing.

  • Gate angle = integer winding × a fixed constant — first-order immune to control-amplitude error; verified two independent ways to 0.1%.
  • Universal. A three-handle device gives two independent generators, so finite winding sequences are dense in SU(2) (Solovay–Kitaev compile).
  • Gap-protected. A dark–bright gap above ~420 MHz keeps thermal and TLS processes from spoiling the protection.
  • A different axis than raw fidelity. The win is robustness and array-size independence, not beating the best demonstrated single-gate number.
The science

Proven on paper, validated in simulation.

The construction, the gauge-invariance proof, and the density-in-SU(2) universality result are worked out analytically and confirmed by multi-mode numerics. It is a design we are looking to prove on a partner's hardware — not yet a fabricated device.

Reference E. S. Brooke, "Universal Holonomic Quantum Gates via Winding-Class-Indexed Dark-State Connections in Multi-Loop Superconducting Circuits" (2026). Gate = exp(i·2πν·Aφ), set by the integer winding number ν.
